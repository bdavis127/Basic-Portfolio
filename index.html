Introduction

My name is Brett Davis. I am an aspiring UX Designer who is currently involved in a coding bootcamp at SMU in Dallas, Texas.

I have had an interest in the art of human - computer interaction since my days of Human Factors Psychology at the University of Central Florida. During my work at the Naval Air Warfare Center in Orlando, I researched methods for improving memory retention of data through user interface design.

While earning my Masters Degree in Instructional Technology at UCF, I was interested in learning how to optimize the rate of learning transfer through Computer Based Training (CBT) as it was called then. I looked for any and all opportunities to further my knowledge in this field, which led to very interesting research, such as the study of Virtual Reality. If only we had the technology then that they have now! It is truly amazing to see the latest VR gadgetry.

My current fascination is web development, most specifically UX Design. I enjoy working with the many aspects of creation involved, such as creating wireframes and prototypes leading up to a finished product based on specific user desires collected through research. I wish to apply my experience and knowledge I have gathered in order to further the abilities of users who desire a positive online experience.

In today's digital environment, the online world is a busy place. Every business and service out there is constantly demanding the attention of potential customers. Proper use of interface design isn't just helpful anymore. It is absolutely necessary. If you are in need of UX design services, please feel free to reach out to me using my Contact page.
